Mental Health Care Accessibility Explorer

Interactive Tools for Analyzing County-Level Mental Health Care Affordability and Availability

This interactive web application provides comprehensive tools to explore mental health care accessibility across U.S. counties. Analyze affordability (AFI) and availability (AVI) metrics, examine relationships between socioeconomic variables, and model different payment scenarios to understand the financial burden of mental health care.

Methodology

Affordability Index (AFI)

The Affordability Index (AFI) measures the financial burden of mental health care as a percentage of disposable income. It is calculated as:

AFI = (Total Out-of-Pocket Cost / Disposable Income) × 100

Where total out-of-pocket cost varies based on the payment scenario (cash payment, copay, deductible with coinsurance, or sliding scale). Higher AFI values indicate greater financial burden. In the calculator tool, AFI values are winsorized at the 5th and 95th percentiles to improve visualization while maintaining data integrity.

Availability Index (AVI)

The Availability Index (AVI) measures the accessibility of mental health care providers in a given geographic area. It reflects the capacity of the local mental health care system to meet population needs, taking into account provider density, population characteristics, and service availability.

Data Categories

Both AFI and AVI are categorized into quintile-based groups for visualization:

Low (0-20th percentile), Low Average (20-40th percentile), Average (40-60th percentile), Above Average (60-80th percentile), and High (80-100th percentile).

For AFI (burden metric), lower values indicate better affordability. For AVI (accessibility metric), higher values indicate better availability.

Data Source: Two Dimensions of Access: Availability and Affordability of Mental Health Care Across the U.S.
